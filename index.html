Hunter Wade York
I am a current fellow at the Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation in Seattle where I model the distributions of educational attainment across the world and how these distributions affect poorer health outcomes. My interests in big data, sociology, and science began with the Natural History of Song project at Harvard in which we described human universals in musical perception. After a pivot to the health sciences, I'm excited to use my expieriences with behavioral lab work and very messy crowdsourced data to inform analyses of large-scale household surveys and linked registry data.

Within social epi, I am most interested in the intergenerational inheritance of socio-economic position, how inequality and relative deprivation can precipitate poor health outcomes, and how social justice can inform my research.
